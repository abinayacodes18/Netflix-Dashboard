# Netflix Content Analysis Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview
This project provides a comprehensive visual analysis of Netflix’s streaming library. Using a dataset of over 5,300 titles, the dashboard identifies patterns in content production, geographical distribution, and audience targeting. 

The goal of this project is to answer key business questions:
* How has the volume of content changed over the last decade?
* Which genres dominate the platform?
* What is the geographic footprint of Netflix's production?
* How does the rating distribution vary between Movies and TV Shows?

## 📊 Dashboard Pages

### 1. Overview Dashboard
The high-level summary of the entire dataset.
* **Key Metrics:** Total shows (5,330), Total Movies (5,185), and TV Shows (147).
* **Content Split:** A donut chart showing that Movies make up ~97% of this specific dataset.
* **Rating Categories:** Breakdown of "Adult" (49%), "Teen" (45%), and "Kids" content.
* **Top Genres:** Visualizing the dominance of International Dramas and Stand-Up Comedy.

### 2. Content Analysis
A deep dive into the quality and creators behind the content.
* **Top 10 Directors:** Tracking the most prolific directors on the platform (e.g., Raúl Campos, Jan Suter).
* **Rating by Type:** A stacked bar chart comparing maturity ratings (TV-MA, TV-14, R, etc.) across Movies and TV Shows.
* **Content Spotlight:** A searchable list featuring top-rated titles like *Stranger Things*, *Fargo*, and *Avatar: The Last Airbender* with their IMDb/Rating scores and plot summaries.

### 3. Audience and Release Trends
Focuses on the "When" and "Where" of Netflix content.
* **Geographic Distribution:** A global map showing the density of titles by country, highlighting North America and Europe as major hubs.
* **Release Velocity:** A timeline chart showing the massive spike in content additions starting around 2016 and peaking in 2019-2020.
* **Genre Popularity:** A horizontal bar chart identifying the top 10 genres by total count.

## 🛠️ Tools Used
* **Power BI / Tableau:** For data visualization and dashboard construction.
* **Dataset:** Netflix Movies and TV Shows dataset (sourced from Kaggle/Internal).
* **Bing Maps API:** For geographic data visualization.

## 💡 Key Insights
* **The 2018 Pivot:** Content additions saw an exponential increase starting in 2017, reaching a peak of nearly 100 titles released in a single window by 2019.
* **Global Reach:** While North America remains the largest contributor, there is significant content density in India, South Korea, and Western Europe.
* **Adult-Centric:** Nearly 50% of the library is categorized as "Adult," suggesting Netflix's primary strategy targets mature audiences.
